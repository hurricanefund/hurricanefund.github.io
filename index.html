Menlo Hurricane Fund

The Menlo Hurricane Fund is a community effort to support
the victims of Hurricane Harvey, Maria, and Irma

We were inspired to create this fund to raise money to support the victims of the hurricane as they are recovering.

What is the fund?

Originally inspired by Hurricane Harvey, members of our Menlo community looked for ways to help. Due to the devestation of Harvey, Irma, and Maria hitting US territory all within of the same month, governmental and independent support has been split between the three affected areas. This has lead to a prolonged recovery from these hurricanes. Please join us in helping those have been affected by the recent natural disasters

How you can help

We set up a Fundraiser page and are graciously accepting donations from the community to help us reach our goal of $20,000 by the end of the school year to help victims of the recent natural disasters. Additionally, we are hoping to engage as much of the Menlo community as we can in this effort and encourage other groups on campus to join us in this fundraising. We encourage all of you to get involved and help us reach this ambitious goal!

Where we’re donating

Although we haven’t identified organizations or other places to donate for all areas affected, the Hurricane Fund is meant to support groups that might not be getting resources. For example, in Houston we have identified a school district, that was underfunded prior to Hurricane Harvey, in which several schools were almost completely destroyed.
